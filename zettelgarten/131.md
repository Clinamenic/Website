> Unlike corporations, which can maintain fairly poor relational practices and still make enough money to pay people to show up, movements face strong evolutionary pressure to build spaces that keep people coming back. Movements that gather relationally attract members and thrive, and movements that fail to do so wither, so in my experience the gatherings that movements create are often radically more relational than the dry networking events and team-building exercises that pass for relationality in the corporate world.
