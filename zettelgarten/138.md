> Shaady Salehi of the Trust-Based Philanthropy project is part of a new wave of philanthropists arguing that deploying resources should center the work of building trust over that of collecting and reviewing grant applications. The latter not only requires an extraordinary amount of time from both grantors and grantees, it forces relational work into a transactional box that often renders it radically less effective. Instead, Salehi and her colleagues assert that funders should center the process of building trust with the communities that they are funding, starting from a place of trust rather than demanding that trust be earned through compliance and reporting.
